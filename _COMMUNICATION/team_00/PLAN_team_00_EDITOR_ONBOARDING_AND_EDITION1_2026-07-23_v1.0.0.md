# Plan — Editor (Tzlil) onboarding + a first edition everyone waits for

## 0. The one idea
**Tzlil (3rd daughter, math-minded) = Editor-in-Chief.** Her two needs — a *smooth
interface* and a *real intellectual challenge* — are the design constraints. We satisfy
both by making editing a **thinking game she owns**, not a chore she approves. Her
signature: the **weekly brain-teaser**. Her power: the **final gate** (nothing publishes
without her — this is also the runtime trigger, per the no-API decision).

## 1. Tzlil's role (the pitch to her)
- **Title + ownership:** "עורכת ראשית — בית ולד 📰". Real credit (already in the footer),
  real authority (the gate). Not helping — *running* it.
- **Her signature = the puzzle.** She creates/curates the weekly brain-teaser (a genuine
  math/logic challenge), answer revealed next week. This is her intellectual challenge AND
  the family's #1 recurring hook.
- **Her editorial judgment:** she decides what's "surprising + quality enough," picks the
  best discovery-bridges, writes a short **הערת העורכת** (editor's note — the human voice).
- **Safe bounds (built-in):** the pipeline's content guardrails already enforce the family
  sensitivities (the NEVER-FOR-NEWSLETTER rules); Nimrod co-owns any sensitive call. So her
  creative freedom runs inside safe rails.
- **The reward loop:** she sees the family react (the survey + one-tap emoji rating in the
  footer). Seeing them love it = the fuel.

## 2. Her interface — "smooth" + "intellectual" (examine + choose)
The runtime = a Cloud agent generates a **draft edition**; Tzlil reviews + decides; her
approval publishes. Three ways she can work (pick one for edition #1, evolve later):
- **(A) Chat-based editorial session (recommended for the intellectual challenge).** Tzlil
  chats with an "editor's assistant" that presents the week's draft + *options* (headline
  picks, 3 puzzle candidates, which bridges) and she **reasons + decides + co-creates the
  puzzle** conversationally. Most engaging for a math kid; smooth; uses our AI stack.
- **(B) Preview + light form.** She sees the rendered draft + a small form (tweak greeting,
  pick puzzle from options, add her note, approve). Smoothest, least "thinking."
- **(C) Guided session (MVP for edition #1).** For the *first* edition: Tzlil + Nimrod sit
  together over the live preview, she makes the calls. Zero build; proves the ritual; then
  we build A.
> **Recommendation:** ship edition #1 on **(C)**, build **(A)** as her real ongoing tool.

## 3. Edition #1 — the anticipation engines (all built; fill with content)
| Engine | Section (built) | Edition-#1 move |
|---|---|---|
| **Personalization that surprises** | per-member corners ×5 | one genuinely "how did it know?" pick per member (from the taste profiles) |
| **Family connection** | discovery-bridges + שולחן שישי | one bridge that sparks a real dinner conversation |
| **Recurring hook** | puzzle (+ next-week reveal) | **Tzlil's debut brain-teaser** — set the bar |
| **Participation** | survey + emoji rating | a fun, low-effort question the whole family answers |
| **Human voice** | הערת העורכת + footer credit | Tzlil's first editor's note — warm, a little cheeky |
| **The mascot** | Skipper Cat (teaser + slots) | **introduce him as a character** — a name/backstory beat |

**Over-index for #1 on three things:**
1. **Skipper Cat's introduction** — a memorable first appearance (a one-line origin / his
   "voice"), so he becomes a friend they look forward to.
2. **"This is OURS"** — inside jokes + family lore (we have raw material in
   `TRANSCRIPT_MINING`) so it feels unmistakably theirs, not a generic feed.
3. **A cliffhanger for #2** — end with a hook ("בשבוע הבא…", the puzzle answer pending, a
   "coming next" teaser) that makes opening edition #2 irresistible.

## 4. Recurring-hook menu (Tzlil's editorial call — real options to examine)
She picks 1–2 to commit to as the newsletter's signature beats:
- **The weekly brain-teaser** (hers — strongest; math + suspense).
- **Skipper Cat's weekly mini-adventure** (a running mascot storyline).
- **Rotating member spotlight** (each week one member goes deeper).
- **A family prediction/poll** (last week's result revealed — participation + suspense).
- **A running gag / lore thread** (an inside-joke that builds).
